    About data on PANC-DB


    1. Do we need to register ourselves to download data?

      2. Ans: YES. Please find the link here

    2. What are the key demographic and clinical conditions that HPAP donors need to meet to be accepted as “cases” for the study?

      1. For T1D Program-

        • T1D patients: ≤ 40 years of age; disease duration ≤ 7 years
          OR
        • Autoantibody-positive (Aab+), non-diabetic donor: ≤ 30 years of age
          OR
        • Donor ≤ 30 years of age; BMI ≤ 30; cause of death related to diabetic ketoacidosis
          OR
        • Age-matched controls


      2. For T2D Program-

        • T2D donors
          • Age 35-60 years
          • BMI 25-40
          • A1C > 6.5
          • Any diabetes duration or treatment

          OR

        • Normal donors
          • Age 35-60 years
          • BMI 25-40
          • A1C < 5.7
    3. How many cases vs controls does HPAP currently have including Type-2 diabetes?


      4. Ans. As of September 2021, there are more than 102 donors in our database (including both T1D and T2D cases and controls). The Time series chart on the Data status page as well as the home page (under "our donors" section) will give you up-to-date count of donors, in different categories i.e. T1D, T2D and No Diabetes.

      Donors currently visible on PANC-DB-

      1. T1D donors- 24 count
      2. T2D donors- 22 count
      3. No Diabetes or No HX diabetes- 56 count

    4. How long does it take a donor to be added to PANC-DB once procured? Are there any baseline requirements to make a new donor available to donors?

      5. Ans:Accepting donor organs into the HPAP program and entering them into our database is a dynamic and ongoing process. Hence, often the total donor count on PANC-DB does not represent total donors accepted or being processed at a given time. For a snap-shot of our procurement process, go here. Typically, new donors are posted on PANC-DB within 14 days after the organs have been procured.

      Once a donor organ is procured by HPAP, it is processed for tissue and isle cell retrieval. The curation team receives some key information from this time to generate a locally unique ID for these samples, which is used to make a blank dataset and made available o the teams to input their assay data (which happens in an ad hoc manner and for many assays happens in batches every few monhs). The time it takes for a new donor to show up on PANC-DB post procurement depends on whether or not we have either some key clinical and demographic information on the donor or ther complete clinical summary. The importan information needed (i.e. a new donor cannot be made visible without these attributes) are HPAP-ID, Age, gender, race, BMI, HbA1c%

    5. Do you have Type-2 diabetes donors as well?

      6. Ans: Yes. Just select the T2D option in the Disease filter on the left side of the Donor Information page on PANC-DB.

    6. Are the controls age matched?

      7. Ans: Not necessarily, but one can age-match or match according to other demographics using our filter. Although, HPAP makes every attempt to obtain age-matched controls as they become available; however, the number of organ donors in the US is small

    7. What is the difference or similarity between the "No Diabetes" cases or "controls" for the T1D and T2D programs?

      8. Ans: The controls differs between the two groups only in age distribution. T1D cases tend to be younger than T2D cases. However, we always planned that some of the controls could be used for comparisions with either T2D or T1D. When selecting controls, investigators have to ensure that the disease and control groups do not differ significantly in age, BMI, and sex.

    8. What are the donor snapshots?

      9. Ans: The donor-cards or snapshots give an brief summary of the most salient features of each donor, such a biographical information, disease and autoantibody status, and islet composition.

    9. Can we compare data between different donors?

      10. Ans: Yes. Data from various donors can be selected and compared depending on the user’s biological question.

    10. What individual data per donor can we currently view on HPAP website?

    Pre-and Post-processing information for all data


    1. Where can I get information regarding sample pre and post processing?

      2. Ans:  Information on equipment used and different processes are covered in the Data Workflow section here  which also has the SOPs for different experiments.

    2. Were the bulk and single cell RNA sequencing performed on total pancreatic islet cells or were any populations sorted for sequencing?

      3. Ans: Bulk RNA seq were done using sorted alpha and sorted beta cells.  scRNA seq used dissociated (not sorted) unsorted islet preparations, which by necessity have different degrees of purity.

    3. Does the scRNA seq data on pancreatic islets include infiltrating immune cells, or only endocrine cells?

      4. Ans: Since scRNA-seq were done using islets that have been dissociated (see response above),  non-endocrine cells (i.e. immune cells and stromal cells),  that are embedded within islets are captured and analyzed as well.

    4. How were the pancreatic islets processed and/or sorted for the scRNA seq analysis?

      5. Ans: There is no sorting for scRNA-seq.  Islets preparations of varying purity were dissociated into single cells  and then  processed for the library.

    5. Is there a list of all the panels and/or markers that have been used for analysis of the samples, specifically for T cell studies?

      6. Ans: Yes, please check it out here in the Data workflow section for Immunobiology.

    6. What pre-processing have the IMC images on PANC-DB have undergone?

      7. Ans: The single-channel images on the website available for download in the Download data section, are original unaltered images. The preselected overlays in the IMC viewer (in the Donor Information page) underwent hot-pixel removal, but not compensation, prior to overlaying of the channels. Note that all IMC data can also be viewed on PancreAtlas via a link from PANC-DB, where the users can select and manipulate individual channels as they see fit.

    7. How is “baseline secretion” and “fold stimulation” calculated?

      8. Ans: Currently, fold stimulation is calculated as follows:
      • Baseline secretion- baseline average of values from first 10 time points (mostly 20-30 mins)
      • Fold stimulation- Local-max values / Lead-in Avg values
      • Local max- MAX value in the 80-100 mins range (80-100 min time points)
      • Lead-in Avg- AVG of values between 60-80 min range (60-80 min time points)

      However, we are in the process of updating the formula that generates these values. Please contact us here and we can get you in touch with the investigators directly if necessary.

    1. How are hormone secretion affected between T1D, Aab+ and controls?

      2. Ans: Check the appropriate filters on the left, and then look at Islet Studies >>> Perifusion data.

    2. How are immune cell distribution differed between T1D, Aab+, and control?

      3. Ans: Check the appropriate filters on the left, then look at Islet Studies >>> IMC data.

    3. How does Endocrine cell count differ between T1D and T2D donors?

      4. Ans: Check the appropriate filters on the left, then look at Islet Studies >>> CyTOF data

    4. How to check sex differences in Islet function?

      5. Ans: Check the appropriate filters on the left, then look at islet studies >>> Islet Physiology studies (Perifusion, Oxygen consumption, Calcium imaging).
