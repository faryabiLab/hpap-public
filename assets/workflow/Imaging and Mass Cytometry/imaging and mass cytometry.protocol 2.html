    2.

  4. Procedure
    5.
    1. Use pressure cooker or heat block to preheat Antigen Retrieval Solution to 95 ℃ before starting
    2. Bake slides in 56 ℃ over for 20 mins
    3. Dewax slides in xylene in the fume hood for 5 mins X 2
    4. Hydrate slides in descending grades of ethanol (100%, 95%, 80%, 70%), 5 mins each
    5. While you are dewaxing and hydrating slides, prepare 50 mL 1x PBS by diluting 10x PBS with Milli-Q water
    6. Rinse slides in PBS for 5 mins
    7. Insert slides with tissues into preheated Retrieval Solution and incubate for 30 min at 95 ℃
    8. Following incubation, remove from pressure cooker or heat block and place the tube containing the retrieval solution and slides on a lab bench and cool to room temperature, approximately 30 mins (or until it reaches room temperature)
    9. Wash the slide with PBS for 10 mins
    10. Use PAP pen to encircle sample
    11. Block with 3&$37; BSA in PBS for 45 mins at RT

      • Note:
      • Use enough blocking solution to cover the section (around 300–500 μL/section)
      • Blocking solution should be diluted from 10% BSA freshly made from powder. Remaining 10% BSA should be aliquoted and stored at -20 ℃ and diluted at the time of use

    12. To prepare the antibody cocktail, calculate total volume of antibodies (at concentrations specific for the assay) and bring up to final volume with 0.5% BSA in PBS

      • Note:
      • Spin Antibody at max speed for 2 minutes and take from the top of the tube to avoid antibody aggregates
      • Add a small volume of single antibodies into a larger volume of diluent

    13. Incubate overnight with antibody cocktail at 4 ℃ in hydration chamber (we use an slide box where the slides rest on the shelf and the bottom is covered by wet paper towel)
    14. Wash in PBS for 8 mins x 2
    15. Stain the tissue with Ir-Intercalator (1:400) in DPBS for 30 mins at RT
    16. Rinse in ddH2O for 5 mins
    17. Air dry the slide for at least 20 mins at RT
