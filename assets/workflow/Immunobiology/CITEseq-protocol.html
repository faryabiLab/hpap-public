HPAP CITEseq: Dual-index 3' HT scRNAseq with Antibody Derived Tags and Hashtag Oligos

Compiled by Vincent Wu and Gregory Golden of Betts Lab
Date submitted: May 2023

  1. Description

    2. This assay simultaneously assesses mRNA and select surface antigen profiles at single-cell resolution. This protocol uses a combination of oligo-labeled antibodies (Biolegend TotalSeqA) and the 3' scRNAseq chemistry (10X Genomics). Furthermore, samples are individually labeled with hash-tagging antibodies (oligo-labeled antibodies targeting β2m and CD298) for multiplexing and increased resolution for multiplet filtering upon superloading of the 10X chip. This protocol utilizes the HT system compatible with the Chromium X instrument, allowing double the output of emulsified cells compared to regular throughput systems. This protocol builds upon the following publications/protocols:

    • Stoeckius et al., Nature Methods 2017
    • Stoeckius et al., Genome Biology 2018
    • BioLegend TotalSeq-A Antibodies and Cell Hashing with 10x Single Cell 3' Reagent Kit v3.1 (Dual Index) Protocol (version from 10/22/21, Betts Lab)
    • 10X Genomics Chromium Next GEM Single Cell HT Reagent Kits v3.1 (Dual Index) - RevB, September 10, 2021

  3. Protocol

    1. Section 1: Cell preparation

      1. Prepare 500 mL fresh R10 media.
      2. Prepare 50 mL of R10 + 10 U/mL
      3. Procure cryopreserved vials of samples of interest, keep on dry ice until ready for thawing.
      4. To a conical tube, add 14 mL of R10 media per 1 mL of cryopreserved sample and label the tube with the respective sample name.
      5. To thaw the samples, partially submerge the cryopreserved sample tubes in a 37'℃ water bath and move the tubes to maintain a warm tube-water interface. Maintain movement and submergence until the sample is thawed enough to slide within the tube.
      6. Immediately place the sample in its respective conical tube. Wash the cryotubes out with 200 μL R10 and place into the conical tube.
      7. Cap the conical tubes and invert to mix the samples.
      8. Spin the samples for 5 minutes at 500 xg.
      9. Decant supernatant and resuspend the samples in R10 + DNaseI and count. Place cell counts below:


      10. Bring the concentration to 2x106 live cells/mL for resting. If necessary, spin the samples at 500 xg for 5 min, and resuspend in the amount of R10 + DNAse1 to achieve 2x106 live cells/mL.
      11. Place samples in TC-treated flasks, and place samples in a 37℃ incubator (5% CO2) for 2 hours to rest.

        12. NOTE: After this point. when spinning live cells, set the centrifuge to 10C to help keep cells viable/slow transcriptional dynamics. KEEP SAMPLES COLD AT ALL TIMES!!.

      12. After resting, spin the cells down at 500 xg for 5 minutes at 10℃.
      13. Carefully pipette off supernatant, and resuspend cells in 1mL ice cold Cell Staining Buffer for low-cell count samples and 3 mL for high-cell count samples. Count cells via trypan blue and automated cell counter (i.e.Countess II).
      14. Place cell counts below (pre-live cell enrichment&41;:


      15. If the viability of the sample is >=90%, proceed to the next step.
        1. If the viability of the sample is <90% and you have >10x10 6 cells per sample, use the Dead Cell Removal kit (StemCell; annexin V labeling to target phosphatidylserine which is exposed on the surface of dying/dead cells).
        2. If the viability of the sample is <90% and you have <10x10 6 cells per sample:
          1. Filter the samples with 70 μm FACS filter tubes. Prime filter with 200 μL Cell Staining Buffer, followed by pipetting the sample through the filter. Then, pipette another 500 μL of cold Cell Staining Buffer onto the filter to flush through remaining cells.

        3. Do the same live cell enrichment for all samples in your experiment!
        4. Place cell counts below (post live cell enrichment):


      16. Take 500k live cells from each sample and place in separate 1.5ml DNA lobind tubes.
      17. Spin down the cells at 200g for 10 mins.
      18. With a 1000μl tip inserted inside a 200μl tip, carefully remove supernatant and resuspend cells in 22.5μl Cell Staining Buffer. Keep cells on ice!
        • Note: it's better to leave a 5 μl than lose cells!
        • Keep cells on ice for all remaining steps


    2. Section 2: Cell staining

      1. Take TotalSeqA v1. cocktail out of 4C and equilibrate at RT for 5 mins. DO NOT OPEN VIAL.
        1. Use 1 cocktail vial per every two samples (ie 8 samples requires 4 vials)

      3. Add 27.5μl of Cell Staining Buffer to each cocktail vial.
      4. Vortex cocktail vials for 10 seconds. Briefly spin down.

      6. Vortex again and spin down at 10k g for 30 seconds at RT.

      8. Spin down at 14k g for 10 mins at 4C.
      9. Add 2.5μl of TruStain FcX to each sample and gently pipet to mix.
      10. Incubate for 10 mins at 4C.
      11. Add 12.5μl of Cell Staining Buffer to each sample tube.
      12. Vortex and spin down hashing antibodies.
      13. Add 0.5μg (1μl( of hashtag antibody to each sample tube. Be sure to take hashtag antibody from the top portion of the vial. Take note which HTO was added to each sample in the table below.

Into the new DNA lo-bind tube, gently push the pipette plunger until you are to the second stop. You will then observe the cell pool sample passing through the filter over a period of ~10 seconds.

    3. Section 3: Modifications to 10X Chip M protocol

      1. Follow the Chip M v3.1 protocol until Step 1.2c.
      2. To superload the chip, we need to respect the volume limit in the chip. For rows 2A and 2B of Chip M, the final volume must be 140μl. As such, the 10X protocol prepares an initial combined mixture of 150μl (10X builds in extra volume into the initial mixture) before having the user load 140ul, thus ensuring 140μl is loaded without having to worry about volumes. For the initial combined mixture, 63.6ul of Master Mix is used per well, granting 86.4μl of space for cells.
        1. Utilize the Cell Suspension Volume Calculator Table to decide how many cells to use.
          1. When referring to your cell pool, use total cell count, not live cell count, as dead cells are emulsed as well as alive.
          2. The Cell Suspension Volume Calculator Table only accounts for one well of Chip M (i.e 2A, not 2A + 2B)
        2. For example, if we want a targeted cell concentration of 17,500 cells/well and our cell stock concentration is at 1500 cells/μl. We would then put in 24.1μl of cells and add 62.3μl of nuclease-free water to the Master Mix for Step 1.2b.
          1. If there are 16 reactions being run, that would require 385.6 μl of cell suspension
          2. Expect a loss in cell concentration during washing and FlowMi filtration; i.e. a drop from 2000 cells/μl to 1200 cells/μl. So, expect to need a larger number of cells. Refer to the "Cell Pooling Calculator (10X)" on the Betts Google Drive to calculate how many cells you should add per sample.
      3. Proceed with the rest of 10X Protocol Step 1 through Step 1.4.
      4. At the end of Step 1.4, the GEMs must be evenly distributed between shared samples tubes (i.e. 1A and 1B) to ensure equal library representation downstream. For example, if there is equal GEM creation between 1a and 1b, then move forward through 10X protocol step 2.1. If there is unequal distribution between 1a and 1b, then from the sample with more GEMs slowly withdraw GEMs over 30 seconds and dispense in the opposite sample tube over 20 seconds.
        1. If 1a has complete/partial GEM emulsions and 1b has no GEMs, then move forward with a blank 1b.
      5. Proceed with the rest of 10X Protocol Step 1 through Step 2.1.
      6. For Step 2.2a, add in the additional reagents to cDNA Amplification Mix as such:
        1. Add in 1μl of 0.2μM ADT PCR additive primer (to create 1X primer concentration)
        2. Add in 1ul of 0.1uM HTO PCR additive primer (to create 1X primer concentration)
          1. Just add 1μl of each 10um primer to the final reaction.
      7. Add 65μl of this amplification mix to the sample for Step 2.2b
        1. Number of cDNA amplification cycles: ______ __
          1. For HT reactions, consider starting at 8 cycles
      8. Proceed with the rest of Step 2.2.
      9. For step 2.3, follow these instructions with the below modifications exactly.
        1. In step 2.3d, DO NOT DISCARD THE SUPERNATANT. Reserve the supernatant in new strip tubes. Clearly label the tubes! This supernatant contains your ADT and HTO libraries. Meanwhile, your beads are binding your full length cDNAs. After the supernatant has been saved, proceed with the rest of the 10X protocol for the bead cleanup and preparation of your cDNA fraction. Follow the below steps for the supernatant.
        2. With this supernatant, add 140ul (thus doing a 2X SPRI cleanup) to the supernatant.
          1. Add 2500μl of SPRI beads to multichannel reservoir just prior to use ( for 16 rxns worth)
        3. Pipet to mix and incubate for 10 min at RT with caps open and no bubbles
          1. During the incubation, create new 80% EtOH using molecular grade H2O
          2. If planning on running Tapestation/Qbit, bring out enough TapeStation strips and allow to come to RT.
          3. Near end of incubation, create 8mL of 80% EtOH (1.6 mL of H2O, 6.4 mL of EtOH) just prior to use (for 16 rxns worth)
        4. Centrifuge briefly and place on magnet in High position.
        5. After beads have separated, remove and discard supernatant.
        6. Add 200ul of 80% EtOH and wait 30 secs.
        7. Discard EtoH.
        8. Repeat steps f-g one more time.
        9. Centrifuge the tube briefly to collect beads and place tube on magnet in Low position.
        10. Once beads have moved, use pipet to remove any remaining EtOH. Dry for 1 minute.
          1. Add 1mL of EB to multichannel reservoir just prior to use (for 16 rxns worth)
        11. Resuspend beads in 46.5μl Buffer EB. Pipet to mix.
        12. Incubate for 2 min at RT.
        13. Place on magnet in Low position.
        14. After beads have separated, collect 46μl of the volume into a strip tube.
        15. For each paired sample, combine the two fractions of 46ul eluent (ie from 1a and 1b) into a 92 ul pool.
        16. Quantify DNA concentration and quality of the RNA and ADT/HTO libraries using the Qbit and Tapestation D5000
          1. Tapestation (start first). Label enough tapestation-compatible 8 strip tubes for all samples +1. Add 2μl of D5000 buffer to each tube. Add 2 μl D5000 ladder to the ladder tube. Add 2 μl of each sample to their respective tube. Save photos of each electropherogram to ascertain sample quality.
          2. Qbit (during Tapestation run): On the top of each tube, label enough Qbit tubes for all samples + 2 standards (Standard 1 and Standard 2) + 1 blank. Create enough Qbit working buffer (1ul dye + 199 μl Qbit bufferμ for all tubes + 1. Add 190μl of buffer to the Standard tubes, and 199μl to all other tubes. Add 1μl of sample to their respective sample tube, or 1μl EB to the Blank. Mix all tubes well via vortex and run on the Qbit.
      10. At Step 2.3m, pool samples from corresponding tubes to form cDNA samples for downstream analyses.
      11. Proceed with the rest of the 10X Chip M protocol as specified for the RNA fraction, see protocol below for ADT/HTO fraction.
        1. Do not freeze RNA/ADT/HTO if quantifying the next day!

    6. Section 6: ADT and/or HTO cleanup

        2. Near end of incubation, create 8mL of 80% EtOH (1.6 mL of H2O,6.4 mL of EtOH) just prior to use (for 16 rxns worth)
