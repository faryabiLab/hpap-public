Stanford protocol for processing Single-cell RNAseq
(only performed for HPAP T2D donors)

Seung Kim lab, Stanford University
Date submitted: August 11, 2021

  1. Plate cells into 384-well lysis plate

    1. Use SONY sorter to plate purified beta or alpha cells (see the Stanford SOP for Human islet sorting) into 384-well plate, one cell per well, containing cell lysis buffer (Picelli S. et al 2014 Nature Protocols 9,171-81). Based on NearIR live/dead stain, gate for live single cells
    2. Spin plate for 30 second at room temperature to collect cell lysis to the bottom of well
    3. Seal the plate and store at −80 ℃ until ready to proceed

  2. Construct libraries with SmartSeq2 full-length RNASeq protocol

    1. Following SmartSeq2 protocol (Picelli S et al 2014 Nature Protocols 9,171-81) to generate full-length cDNA library for each cell
    2. Dual indexing strategy was implemented with unique bar codes on both ends
    3. Quality check pooled cDNA library using Bioanalyzer high-sensitivity chips

  3. Perform 100 bp pair-ended sequencing on Illumina NovaSeq6000
