Single-cell ATAC sequencing

Kaestner lab, University of Pennsylvania
Date submitted: August 13, 2020

  1. Steps in pre-processing

    1. Transfer handpicked islets (approximately 5,000 IEQs) into 15 ml conical tube
    2. Add 10 ml of 1 x PBS w/o Ca2+, Mg2+ (Rockland, MB-008). Centrifuge for 2 mins at RT, 180 xg. Aspirate the supernatant
    3. Add 1 ml of warm (37 ℃) 0.05% Trypsin (Invitrogen, 25300054) to the islets. Pipette up and down with p1000
    4. Incubate at 37 ℃ for 9 mins, or until cells are in single cells. Pipette up and down at t = 7 mins, 4 mins, 2 mins, 0 min
    5. Stop the trypsin reaction by adding 1 ml of 100% FBS (Hyclone, SH3091003) to the dissociated islets and pass cells through BD FACs tube with strainer top (Corning 352235)
    6. Use 1 ml of 100% FBS to rinse the tube and pass through the strainer
    7. Transfer cells to 15 ml conical. Centrifuge 4 mins, 400 xg
    8. Remove the supernatant and wash cells with PBS with 10% FBS. Centrifuge for 4 mins, 400 xg
    9. Wash the cells with PBS with 10% FBS and centrifuge for 4 mins, 400 xg. Remove the supernatant
    10. Count cells using a countess chamber
    11. For the scATAC-seq, do the final resuspension in 0.04% BSA in PBS as is consistent with the instructions for using fresh cell in the Nuclei Isolation Protocol
