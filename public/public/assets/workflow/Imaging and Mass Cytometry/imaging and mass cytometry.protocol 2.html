IMC STAINING FOR PARAFFIN SECTIONS
(adapted from Fluidigm PN101-5685-A1)

  1. Purpose
    2.

    This SOP describes the paraffin sections staining procedure for Imaging Mass Cytometry.

  2. List of Equipment
    3.
    1. Slide holder
    2. Slide container
    3. Fume hood
    4. Pressure cooker or heat block (95oC)
    5. PAP Pen
    6. Centrifuge

  3. List of Consumable Reagents
    4.

    Materials Required

    Material Cat No.

    Quantity

    m-Xylene

    185566-1L, Sigma-Aldrich®

    50 mL per 5–7 slides

    Anhydrous Ethyl Alcohol

    USP+432526

    172.5 mL per 5–7 slides

    Antigen Retrieval Reagent Basic

    10mM Tris, 1mM EDTA,
    pH 9.2

    4 mL per 2 slides

    10x PBS, pH 7.2

    MB-008, Rockland

    50 ml per 5-7 slides

    Iridium DNA Intercalator

    201192B, Fluidigm

    2µL per slide

    10% Bovine Serum Albumin (BSA)

    BSA-50 Rockland Immunochemicals

    300µL per slide

  4. Procedure
    5.
  1. Use pressure cooker or heat block to preheat Antigen Retrieval Solution to 95 °C before starting.
  2. Bake slides in 56oC over for 20 min.
  3. Dewax slides in xylene in the fume hood for 5 min X 2.
  4. Hydrate slides in descending grades of ethanol (100%, 95%, 80%, 70%), 5 min each.
  5. While you are dewaxing and hydrating slides, prepare 50 mL 1x PBS by diluting 10x PBS with Milli-Q water.
  6. Rinse slides in PBS for 5 min.
  7. Insert slides with tissues into preheated Retrieval Solution and incubate for 30 min at 95oC.
  8. Following incubation, remove from pressure cooker or heat block and place the tube containing the retrieval solution and slides on a lab bench and cool to room temperature, approximately 30 min (or until it reaches room temperature).
  9. Wash the slide with PBS for 10 min.
  10. Use PAP pen to encircle sample.
  11. Block with 3% BSA in PBS for 45 mins at RT.

    • Note:
    • Use enough blocking solution to cover the section (around 300–500 μL/section).
    • Blocking solution should be diluted from 10% BSA freshly made from powder. Remaining 10% BSA should be aliquoted and stored at −20 °C and diluted at the time of use.

  12. To prepare the antibody cocktail, calculate total volume of antibodies (at concentrations specific for the assay) and bring up to final volume with 0.5%BSA in PBS.

    • Note:
    • Spin Antibody at max speed for 2 minutes and take from the top of the tube to avoid antibody aggregates.
    • Add a small volume of single antibodies into a larger volume of diluent.

  13. Incubate overnight with antibody cocktail at 4˚C in hydration chamber (We use an slide box where the slides rest on the shelf and the bottom is covered by wet paper towel).
  14. Wash in PBS for 8 min x 2.
  15. Stain the tissue with Ir-Intercalator (1:400) in DPBS for 30 min at RT.
  16. Rinse in ddH2O for 5 min.
  17. Air dry the slide for at least 20 minutes at RT.
